[ 01 ] THE CUTTING ROOM

The hours that never made it into the cut.

Editors don't lose hours to the creative work. They lose them to the before — scrubbing 90-minute interviews to find a 7-second line. Logging takes. Hunting selects in a folder named "FINAL_v17_ACTUAL".

// 01

90 minutes to find seven seconds.

Every interview, every doc, every podcast. The line is in there — but it's somewhere between take 02 and take 14. You scrub.

// 02

The EP waits.

You're on the timeline. They want next week's sequence pulled. They can't touch your project. They wait, or they badly recreate the work.

// 03

Tools that live outside Premiere.

Every AI editing app on the market is a separate site, a separate export, a separate round-trip. Your timeline never sees it.

// 04

The first cut eats the brief.

By the time the rough is done, the deadline is gone. The fun part — actually shaping the story — happens at 2am, on a Friday.

[ 02 ] HOW IT CUTS

Inside Premiere. Beside the timeline.

A panel that lives where the work lives. No round-trips, no exports, no separate browser. The AI watches the bin, the timeline, the transcripts — and gives back hours.

01

Ingest & listen.

Drop the rushes into your bin. CineSmithPro transcribes, speaker-labels, and tags every take — searchable in seconds.

02

Ask for the beat.

Type "find the moment she talks about her father" — it surfaces every variant across every take, with in/out points ready.

03

Drop & cut.

Hand off the rough assembly. Send-to-timeline. Drag, trim, lift, shuffle — exactly the way you already work, with the hours given back.
